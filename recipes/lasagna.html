Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a dish made of stacked layers of pasta alternating with fillings such as rag√π, cheese, vegetables, and seasonings such as garlic, oregano, basil, cinnamon, and nutmeg. The dish is popular in many countries, and is commonly made with tomatoes, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese. It is often baked in an oven. Lasagna is a type of pasta, and is typically made with sheets of pasta called lasagne, which are cut into squares or rectangles. The word lasagna is the plural form of the Italian lasagna, which means "flat sheets of pasta".

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (26 ounce) jar spaghetti sauce
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 (15 ounce) container ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano

Recipe

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a large pot, bring 4 quarts water to a boil. Add 1 tablespoon salt and lasagna noodles. Cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  3. In a large skillet, cook and stir ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until meat is brown and onion is tender. Drain excess grease. Stir in tomato sauce, water, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer, uncovered, for 10 minutes.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, and 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese. Spread 1/3 of the meat sauce in the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Top with 3 noodles, 1/3 of the ricotta mixture, and 1/3 of the remaining meat sauce. Repeat layers twice. Top with remaining 3 noodles and meat sauce. Sprinkle with remaining mozzarella cheese.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Let stand for 15 minutes before serving.
